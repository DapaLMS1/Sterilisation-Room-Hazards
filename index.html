Common Hazards in the Dental Practice

Click on each hazard to reveal the risks and controls.

Risk:

Puncture wounds from contaminated instruments during transport, cleaning, and packaging.

Controls:

  • Use puncture-proof transport containers.
  • Wear puncture-resistant, chemical-resistant utility gloves for all instrument handling.
  • Use long-handled brushes for manual cleaning.
  • Never reach into sinks or ultrasonic cleaners blindly.

Risk:

Skin/eye irritation or respiratory issues from disinfectants, instrument detergents, and sterilants.

Controls:

  • Wear appropriate PPE (utility gloves, masks, protective eyewear/face shield).
  • Ensure adequate ventilation in the reprocessing area.
  • Follow manufacturer's instructions for dilution and use.
  • Know the location of and how to use the Safety Data Sheet (SDS).

Risk:

Burns from hot instruments, chamber walls, or escaping steam when opening the autoclave.

Controls:

  • Stand to the side of the door, not in front of it, when opening.
  • Open the autoclave door slightly to allow steam to vent before fully opening.
  • Allow instrument packs to cool completely before handling.
  • Use heat-resistant gloves if you must handle hot cassettes.

Risk:

Musculoskeletal injuries from repetitive motions, handling heavy cassettes, and awkward postures.

Controls:

  • Organise workflow into a logical flow to minimise twisting and reaching.
  • Use height-adjustable stools and benches where possible.
  • Take regular micro-breaks to stretch and change position.
  • Lift heavy instrument cassettes with your legs, not your back.

Risk:

Falling on wet floors from splashes during instrument cleaning or from spills.

Controls:

  • Clean up all spills immediately.
  • Ensure the area has non-slip flooring.
  • Wear appropriate closed-toe, non-slip footwear.
  • Keep walkways clear of clutter and obstacles.
